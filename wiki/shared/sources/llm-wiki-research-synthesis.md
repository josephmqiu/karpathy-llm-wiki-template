# Research Synthesis: LLM-Maintained Personal Wiki Patterns

## Summary

A research subagent survey of the community response to [[andrej-karpathy]]'s [[karpathy-llm-wiki-gist|gist]], conducted during the v1 planning phase of this wiki. Covers reference implementations, PKM adaptations, CLAUDE.md conventions that have converged, multi-topic vault organization, and key pitfalls.

## Provenance

Generated by a general-purpose research subagent on 2026-04-09. This is a self-authored raw source — not an external article — preserved verbatim as the wiki's second ingest and a system validation test.

## Community adoption

The gist hit 5,000+ GitHub stars and the HN front page (158 points, 45+ comments) within days of its April 3, 2026 release. The pattern is now widely referenced as "the LLM Wiki pattern" or "idea file."

[[andrej-karpathy]]'s own reported result: one research topic grew to ~100 articles, ~400K words, without him writing the prose himself.

## Reference implementations surveyed

- **MehmetGoekce/llm-wiki** — L1/L2 cache architecture (L1 = rules/gotchas auto-loaded every session; L2 = wiki loaded on demand), Logseq + Obsidian adapters, bootstrap script
- **NicholasSpisak/second-brain** — Obsidian-first, clean folder layout with ingest/query/lint skills
- **AgriciDaniel/claude-obsidian** — adds `/autoresearch` and a "hot cache" pattern (`wiki/hot.md`) for session continuity
- **Ss1024sS/LLM-wiki** — multi-platform: syncs CLAUDE.md, AGENTS.md, .cursorrules, .windsurfrules from one spec
- **LLM Wiki v2 gist (Rohit G.)** — extends Karpathy's with confidence scoring, supersession, retention decay, typed entity graphs, hybrid BM25+vector+graph search

## Patterns that have converged across implementations

1. **Page-type taxonomy**: entity / concept / source / synthesis — now standard
2. **Frontmatter fields**: `type`, `sources`, `related`, `created`, `updated`, `confidence`
3. **Log prefix**: `## [YYYY-MM-DD] ingest | Title`
4. **10-15 page ripple rule** per ingest (at scale; early ingests should be lighter)
5. **Compile-first rule** (Ss1024sS): every decision written back to wiki, not just delivered in chat
6. **One MOC per topic**, entities live once in shared space, linked from multiple MOCs
7. **Chapter-level splitting** of long sources before ingest

## PKM patterns adapted for LLMs

- **Zettelkasten + LLM**: A-Mem paper (arXiv:2502.12110) frames agentic memory as Zettelkasten-derived. Tension: atomic notes suit LLM reading, but the cognitive benefit of Zettelkasten comes from the human writing them.
- **PARA + LLM**: Common top-level scheme, but most implementations replace the Resources layer with the wiki sub-tree (concepts/, entities/, sources/, synthesis/).
- **Multi-topic consensus**: don't go deeper than two folder levels; use MOCs and wikilinks for cross-topic discovery. Type-first at top level, MOCs for contextual views.

## Key pitfalls identified

1. **Error compounding** — mistakes in early ingests get backlinked into later pages; human review of lint output is non-optional. Karpathy recommends ingesting one source at a time and staying involved.
2. **Source granularity** — the most successful HN reporter split 3 books into chapter-level files and got 210 concept pages + 4,597 cross-references. Naive whole-book ingests produced "slop."
3. **Markdown-as-database limits** — no querying, no relationship traversal, no concurrent writes, inconsistent schema, scale ceiling at ~100-300 sources. Mitigation: strict frontmatter, frequent linting, accept the ceiling or add Dataview/SQLite.
4. **Cognitive offloading** — wikis written entirely by the LLM bypass the mental-model-building that made note-taking useful. Effective users treat the LLM as "the bookkeeper, not the thinker."
5. **Context window degradation** — starts around 200-300K tokens even in 1M-context models; wiki-in-context doesn't scale indefinitely.

## Open debates

- **"Is this just RAG?"** — defenders: compiled wiki returns structured, cross-referenced, deduplicated knowledge vs. raw chunks. Detractors: extra steps that context window growth will make obsolete.
- **Model collapse vs. compounding** — Shumailov et al. 2024 Nature paper on collapse was cited; counter: well-curated LLM-on-LLM cycles have delivered wins in practice.
- **Human-in-the-loop degree** — Karpathy's "one at a time, stay involved" vs. fully automated pipelines with scheduled hooks.
- **Markdown-only vs. markdown + DB** — personal-scale users say markdown is enough; power users at 1000+ sources reach for SQLite/Kuzu.
